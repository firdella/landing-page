    Material or rubbish such as piles of paper, cardboard, and other combustible materials if disposed of irregularly can increase the potential for fire. Moreover, there is a source of flame that will quickly burn the material. You should not store flammable materials at work or keep flammable materials away from sources of ignition.

  2. Liquid and Gasses

    Liquids and gases are a threat in some workplaces such as industries that produce ethylene, oil, etc. Also, the kitchen or office pantry usually stores flammable liquids and gas. If these materials are connected with heat, then not only fire but also an explosion will occur. Therefore, to reduce the risk of fire, always ensure that flammable liquids are stored and sealed and always cleaned if there are spills.

  3. Electrical Equipment

    In general, electrical equipment is often a trigger for fire. Electrical equipment and heat engines have the potential to burn more easily and quickly if you do not unplug the equipment used. For this reason, do the monitor regularly at your workplace for signs of loose and chipped cable, spark plugs damaged or damaged equipment that can trigger an electrical short circuit.
